# Acme Bank — Customer Complaint Handling Procedure

**Policy ID:** ACME-POL-CCH-004
**Owner:** Director of Compliance
**Review cadence:** Annual

> This is a synthetic sample policy for an imaginary institution ("Acme Bank").
> It is not derived from any real company's material.

## 1. Purpose

This procedure establishes Acme Bank's standards for receiving, acknowledging,
investigating, resolving, and reporting customer complaints. The goal is to
identify root causes of customer harm, remediate affected customers promptly,
and feed systemic findings back into first-line product and operations teams.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all Acme Bank employees, contractors, and third-party
service providers who receive or process communications from consumer or small
business customers. It covers complaints received through any channel,
including branch, phone, online banking message, email, social media, CFPB
Consumer Complaint Database, state attorney general referrals, and
Better Business Bureau submissions.

## 3. Definitions

- **Complaint:** Any expression of dissatisfaction from a customer regarding a
  product, service, fee, employee, or process offered by Acme Bank, whether or
  not the customer uses the word "complaint."
- **Regulatory complaint:** Any complaint received through a federal or state
  regulator, a consumer reporting agency dispute, or an attorney demand letter.
- **Root cause:** The underlying process, system, control, or policy defect
  that produced the customer impact.

## 4. Intake and Acknowledgement

### 4.1 Logging

Every complaint must be logged in the Complaint Management System (CMS) within
one (1) business day of receipt. The intake record must capture: customer
identifier, channel, product affected, complaint narrative (verbatim where
feasible), receipt date, and the employee who logged it.

### 4.2 Acknowledgement

- Standard complaints: written acknowledgement to the customer within three
  (3) business days.
- Regulatory complaints: written acknowledgement within one (1) business day
  and an internal notification to the Regulatory Response team.

## 5. Triage and Severity

Complaints are assigned a severity level at intake:

| Severity | Criteria                                                  | Resolution SLA |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| S1       | Alleged UDAAP harm, discrimination, or regulatory inquiry | 15 days        |
| S2       | Monetary impact > $500 or recurring pattern flagged       | 20 days        |
| S3       | Service-quality or single-transaction issue               | 30 days        |
| S4       | General feedback, no action required                      | Acknowledge only |

Severity may only be downgraded by a Compliance Manager with written
justification in the CMS record.

## 6. Investigation

The assigned investigator must:

1. Review the complaint narrative and any supporting documentation.
2. Pull all relevant account activity, call recordings, and system logs.
3. Interview the employee(s) involved where applicable.
4. Determine whether a Regulation-specific issue is implicated — including,
   but not limited to, Regulation E (electronic fund transfers),
   Regulation Z (truth in lending), Regulation B (equal credit opportunity),
   or the Fair Credit Reporting Act.
5. Document findings and the proposed resolution in the CMS.

## 7. Resolution and Remediation

Resolutions must be substantive. A complaint is not resolved merely because
the customer has stopped responding.

- Monetary remediation is issued through the standard reimbursement workflow,
  with the root cause code attached so Finance can reconcile the expense.
- Non-monetary remediation (process fix, apology letter, account restriction
  removal) must be confirmed in writing to the customer.
- Any remediation above $2,500 requires Compliance Manager review before
  disbursement.

## 8. Root Cause Analysis

For every S1 complaint and every recurring S2 pattern, the investigator opens
a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) case. The RCA assigns a root cause to one of the
following categories:

- Policy gap
- Procedure gap
- System defect
- Training deficiency
- Human error (non-systemic)
- Third-party service provider issue

Systemic root causes are escalated to the Compliance Committee within ten
(10) business days of RCA closure.

## 9. Reporting

- **Monthly:** Complaint volume, aging, severity mix, top five root causes,
  and regulatory complaint trend — distributed to Compliance Committee.
- **Quarterly:** Regulatory complaint summary distributed to the Board Risk
  Committee.
- **Annually:** Comprehensive complaint program review including SLA
  performance, remediation spend, and thematic findings.

## 10. Retention

All complaint records are retained for a minimum of seven (7) years from the
date of resolution, or longer if required by applicable regulation or
litigation hold.
